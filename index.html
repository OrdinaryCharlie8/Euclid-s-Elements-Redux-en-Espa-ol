If the segments $\overline{AF}$and $\overline{BF}$ are constructed, prove that the figure $\boxdot ACBF$ is a rhombus. \begin{figure}[H] \centering \includegraphics{\string"Latex/Illustrator/Book 1/Proposition 1.1/Exercises/Proposition 1.1,#1\string".pdf} \caption{} \end{figure} \end{xca} \begin{proof} Because $A$ is the center of the circle $\Circle A$, $\overline{AF}=\overline{AB}$ {[}Def. 1.33{]}. Because $B$ is the center of the circle $\Circle B$, $\overline{BF}=\overline{AB}$ {[}Def. 1.33{]}. Since $\overline{AF}=\overline{BF}=\overline{AB}=\overline{AC}=\overline{BC}$, it follows that $\boxdot ACBF$ is a rhombus.
